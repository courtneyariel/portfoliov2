Overview

Building my portfolio meant a lot to me because I was finally able to come back to one of my childhood passions, designing and coding. Determined to make my passion into a career, I became an apprentice at DESIGNATION Labs and worked on projects to get a better understanding of UX/UI and front-end development. Now with my projects complete it was time to put together a portfolio of my work.

Client

Myself

Role

UX, Wireframe, UI, Development

Process

Research, Wireframe, Style Tiles, High Fidelity Mockups, Development

The Goal

Create and develop an online portfolio for my work.

UX

In order to build my portfolio I first looked to designers I admired and see how they organized their portfolios.

From doing this I gathered that:

  • - What content I should and should not have on my portfolio
  • - Different ways of organizing my content
  • - Make my portfolio and extension of myself, allow it to showcase who I am as a person
  • - Rather than showing my final design, I noticed it would be better to explain my design thinking process and represent my projects as case studies so the person viewing my project would have a better understanding as to how I made my design decisions and came to the final design.

Wireframes

After researching portfolios, I began to mockup a wireframe in Sketch of how I would like my portfolio to look.

It made sense to include the navigation at the top and then have it positioned fixed in CSS so the user could navigate easily once on the page. I then wanted to include the about section next so the user would understand the purpose of the site and get a brief intro to me as a person.

Style Tiles

Now that I had my wireframe laid out I created some style tiles. I wanted a somewhat elegant and modern look.

High Fidelity Mockups

At first I had wanted to go with the pink, black, and grey design. I wanted to try to incorporate a background picture that represented Korea because I love Korean culture and the language, however I could not find anything that fit with my design or my personality. I also felt the first design could use some refinement.

This led to me back to my word map and I decided get in touch with the holistic side of myself that loves eating healthy and to connect with nature. I found the background image I'm using currently and fell in love with it and wanted to use it right away. I then went into Photoshop and played around with different colors and styles until I decided on using the purple, green, and blue tones.

Development

After choosing the second design I began to code the design in Sublime with HTML, CSS, and JQuery. I made it responsive so it will be easily viewable on mobile, tablet, and desktop. I also changed the font from Futura to Lato because I was not sure if I wanted all of the body text to be geometric. If there are any errors please let me know!
